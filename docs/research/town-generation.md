# Deep dive: 3D town generation from 2D reference

Fully verified 2026-07-16 (108 agents, 7 synthesized findings, all high-confidence;
1 claim refuted). The pipeline below is now confirmed end-to-end against primary
sources.

## The confirmed pipeline: roads → parcels → massing → roofs
1. **Road growth** — Parish & Müller (SIGGRAPH 2001, the CityEngine lineage):
   L-systems extended with global goals + local constraints, driven entirely by
   **2D image maps** (land-water boundaries, population density, elevation) as
   constraint layers. Verifier caveats worth keeping: later analyses (Kelly &
   McCabe 2006; Barrett) note the mechanism is functionally a *priority-queue
   growth algorithm* with the L-system formalism largely incidental — implement
   the queue, not the grammar — and the image maps condition growth, they don't
   replicate exact street plans.
   Alternatives, both confirmed: **Citygen** (2007) — terrain-adaptive primary
   roads (minimum-elevation / least-elevation-difference sampling), city cells
   via Minimum Cycle Basis, secondary growth + snapping, 24k buildings in 3.5 s
   on 2007 hardware, real-time designer steering (by manually tracing primary
   roads, not map import); and **tensor field + multi-agent tracing** (Zhang &
   Shi 2022), where planning indices (road density, intersection spacing)
   constrain the agents.
2. **Parcels** — Vanegas et al. (Eurographics 2012, shipped in CityEngine): the
   canonical two-pronged method — **straight-skeleton (offset) subdivision** for
   irregular/organic blocks, **OBB recursive subdivision** for rectilinear ones;
   demonstrated to statistically resemble real parcel patterns across styles.
   **Refuted (0-3):** the claim that the parcel stage *guarantees* buildable
   lots — validity for downstream massing is a goal, not a guarantee; keep a
   rejection/repair pass after subdivision.
3. **Massing** — CGA shape grammars (Müller et al., SIGGRAPH 2006): building
   shells from footprints with arbitrary-orientation volumes; demonstrated by
   **rebuilding Pompeii from its historical 2D plan** — the direct academic
   precedent for "generate a 3D settlement matching a known 2D chart" (real
   footprints constrain layout, probabilistic rules extrude plausible massing).
4. **Roofs** — the **straight skeleton is a solved primitive**: skeleton of a
   footprint = hip-roof layout (lift edges by offset distance, drainage-correct);
   weighted variants (Held & Palfrader 2017) give per-edge slopes for hip/gable/
   asymmetric mixes; CGAL ships `extrude_skeleton()` (holes, per-edge angles,
   height-capping for mansards) and a **WebAssembly wrapper exists** for browser
   use. Confirmed 3-0 across six claims.

## Preindustrial morphology (the Annals' era)
Bulbul, *Graphical Models* 2023: towns generated by six user-weighted **semantic
principles** — security, privacy, sustainability, social life, economy, beauty —
via site selection then iterative growth, explicitly producing organic
preindustrial settlements rather than grids. Philosophically the closest model
to how the sim already grows villages; a principled source of weights.

## How this maps onto index.html
The sim already does terrain-conditioned placement, A*-grown roads, radial
streets, merged-geometry buildings. Confirmed-next steps by value:
1. Skeleton-offset parcel subdivision inside street blocks (organic layouts),
   with a validity/repair pass (per the refuted-guarantee finding).
2. Straight-skeleton roofs over footprints (cheap in JS for convex quads).
3. Chart-conditioned street growth for canon cities: use Epēshu's districts/
   quays as Parish-Müller-style constraint image maps.

## Open questions the dive could not settle
- Watabou's Medieval Fantasy City Generator / Emil's generators: no verified
  claims on their internals survived (likely Voronoi/ward partitioning — would
  need source reading, not literature search).
- Quantitative preindustrial morphology (market squares, walls, harbors as
  growth attractors) beyond Bulbul's six principles.
- *Exact* reproduction of a known 2D chart (vectorize the chart into a primary
  road graph Citygen-style, generate only secondaries?) vs. conditioning.
- Whether post-2022 learned methods (diffusion street graphs, neural parcels)
  beat the classical pipeline, and whether any run in no-build vanilla JS.
